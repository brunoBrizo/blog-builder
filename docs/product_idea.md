# AI & Tech Blog

We are building an AI & Tech blog to rank fast in Google so we can get many visits and be able to monetize it with Google Ads.

## 1\. SEO & Technical Requirements to Rank Fast

## Technical SEO (do before launching)

Based on the 2026 SEO checklist from seoClarity and Kleverish's ranking guide:

- HTTPS — mandatory, no exceptions
- Mobile-first design — Google indexes mobile version first. LCP under 2.5 seconds on mobile
- XML Sitemap — auto-generated and submitted to Google Search Console
- Robots.txt — properly configured
- Core Web Vitals — LCP \< 2.5s, FID \< 100ms, CLS \< 0.1
- Schema markup — Article schema, FAQ schema, and How-To schema on every post. Can boost CTR by up to 30%
- Clean URL structure — yourdomain.com/how-to-use-chatgpt-for-coding not yourdomain.com/post-123
- Internal linking — every new post links to 2–3 related posts. Build topic clusters around cornerstone content
- Google Search Console \+ GA4 — set up day one

## On-Page SEO (every article)

- One H1 (title) with primary keyword near the beginning
- H2/H3 subheadings with secondary keywords
- Meta description under 155 characters with primary keyword
- Alt text on all images
- 1,500–2,500 words per article
- Target long-tail, low-difficulty keywords — this is critical for a new domain

## International SEO (hreflang)

For multilingual, implement hreflang tags via XML sitemap (best for performance). Use the structure:

`yourdomain.com/en/article-slug  (English - default)`  
`yourdomain.com/pt/article-slug  (Portuguese)`  
`yourdomain.com/es/article-slug  (Spanish)`

Always include an x-default tag pointing to the English version for undefined regions. Use subdirectories (/en/, /pt/) over subdomains — they consolidate domain authority into one domain instead of splitting it.

## Backlinks (the make-or-break factor)

This is what separates blogs that survive from those that

[disappear after 3 months](https://ppc.land/se-ranking-study-shows-ai-content-disappears-from-search-after-3-months/)

. The proven methods for 2025–2026:

1. Guest posting — search "your niche \+ write for us" and pitch articles to relevant blogs with one link back to your site
2. Skyscraper technique — find popular articles with lots of backlinks, create a better version, reach out to sites linking to the original
3. HARO/Connectively — respond to journalist queries as an expert, get quoted with a backlink from major publications
4. Original data/research — publish surveys, benchmarks, or unique analysis. These naturally attract backlinks

Target: build 5–10 quality backlinks per month during the first 6 months. The

[$3,674/month case study](https://www.reddit.com/r/passive_income/comments/1dezu8y/0_to_3674m_in_14_months_auto_ai_content_site/)

built 75 backlinks in 14 months (\~5/month).

## 2\. Blog Sections & Pages

## Mandatory Pages (for AdSense approval)

These are non-negotiable — your AdSense application will be rejected without them:

1. Homepage — clear value proposition, featured/latest articles, newsletter CTA
2. About page — your real name, stated expertise (full-stack dev, SaaS builder), photo. Critical for E-E-A-T
3. Contact page — contact form \+ email address
4. Privacy Policy — required by AdSense and GDPR
5. Terms & Conditions
6. Disclaimer — especially important for tech content (affiliate disclosure, etc.)

## Blog Structure

Based on Productive Blogging's site structure guide, aim for 4–8 main categories. For an AI/Tech blog:

1. AI Tools & Reviews — tool comparisons, tutorials, "best X for Y"
2. Development & Coding — programming tutorials, framework guides, dev tips
3. Tech News & Trends — new releases, industry updates (great for freshness)
4. Productivity & Automation — workflows, efficiency tools, automation guides
5. Guides & Tutorials — in-depth how-to content (your cornerstone/pillar content)

Each category should have one cornerstone article (3,000+ word definitive guide) that all related posts link back to. This tells Google which content is most important.

## Additional Sections

- Newsletter signup — on every page, sticky. Send a weekly digest email to every subscribed user listing the articles published that week (one digest per locale the user subscribed in, so `/pt-br` subscribers get the Portuguese versions, etc.). Every email must include a one-click unsubscribe link (compliant with CAN-SPAM and GDPR) and the `List-Unsubscribe` and `List-Unsubscribe-Post` headers required by Gmail/Yahoo bulk-sender rules
- Search functionality — essential for content-heavy sites
- Language switcher — prominent in header for your multilingual setup
- Related articles — at the bottom of every post (increases time on site, reduces bounce rate)

## AdSense Approval Timeline

- Minimum to apply: 15–30 quality articles, each 800+ words, all indexed in Google
- Domain age: at least 30 days old before applying
- Approval time: typically 5–9 days once you apply
- Keep posting during review — publish 2+ articles per week during the review period
- Zero empty pages — no "coming soon" placeholders, no empty categories

## Content Refresh Strategy

Updating old posts is 2–3x more effective than publishing new thin content. Build a cron job that also refreshes your top-performing articles monthly — this signals freshness to Google and keeps rankings stable.
